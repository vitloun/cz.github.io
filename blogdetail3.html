How to prepare the perfect
fries in an air fryer



Ingredient

Making crispy, golden fries in an air fryer is simple! Here's a step-by-step guide for perfect air-fried fries:

  • 2–3 medium-sized potatoes (Russet or Yukon Gold work best)
  • 1–2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Optional seasonings: pepper, paprika, garlic powder, or herbs like rosemary

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the Potatoes: Peel the potatoes (optional) and cut them into evenly sized fries (about 1/4 inch thick). Keeping them niform ensures they cook evenly.
  2. Soak the Potatoes: Place rhe cut potatoes in a bowl of cold water for at least 30 minutes. This remove excess starch, resulting in cripier fries.
  3. Drain and Dry: Drain the soaked potatoes and pat them completely dry with a clean towel or paper towels. Removing excess moisture is key to getting them crispy.
  4. Toss with oil: In a bowl, toss the dry fries with olive oil, making sure they are evenly coated. You can also add salt and favorite seasonings at this point.
  5. Preheat the Air Fryer: Preheat your air fryer to 375°F (190°C) for about 3–5 minutes
  6. Cook the fries: Place the fries in the air fryer basket in a single layer (do not overcrowd). Fry them for 15–20 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through to ensure even cooking. You may need to cook them in batches depending on your air fryer’s capacity.
  7. Crips and Check: If you want extra crispy fries, increase the temperature to 400°F (200°C) and cook for an additional 3–5 minutes. Check for doneness and adjust the time based on how crispy you like them.
  8. Serve: Remove the fries, sprinkle with a bit more salt if needed, and serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce!

Tips

  • For extra crispiness, after drying the potatoes, sprinkle them with a bit of cornstarch before adding oil.
  • Thinner fries will cook faster, so adjust the cooking time based on thickness.
