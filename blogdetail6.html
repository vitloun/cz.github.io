How to prepare delicious
Beef Steak



Ingredient

Cooking a delicious beef steak is all about using the right technique and seasoning to bring out the natural flavors of the meat. Here's a guide to cooking a perfect steak, whether you like it rare, medium, or well done.

  • 1 beef steak (ribeye, sirloin, filet mignon, or New York strip)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1-2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2-3 sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions

  1. Choose the right steak:
    • Opt for steaks about 1-1.5 inches thick, with good marbling marbling (fat distribution) for tenderness and flavor.
  2. Prepare the steak:
    • Take the steak out of the refrigerator at least 30-60 minutes before cooking to allow it to come to room temperature. this ensures even cooking.
    • Pat the steak dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
    • Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both side. Let it sit for a few minutes to absorb the seasoning.
  3. Preheat the pan:
    • Heat a heavy skillet (caast iron work great) over high heeat for a few minutes until it's smooking hot. The pan needs to be very hot to get a good sear.
  4. Sear the Steak:
    • Add 1-2 teaspoons of olive oil to the pan. AS soon as the oil start shimmering, carefully place the steak in the pan.
    • Sear the steak without moving it for about 2-3 minutes on each side, depending on the thickness of the steak. You want a nice crust to form. Use tongs to flip it when ready.
    • For thicker steaks, don't forget to also sear the side for about 30 seconds.
  5. Add Aromatics:
    • When you flip the steak, add the butter, smashed garlic cloves, and rosemary or thyme sprigs to the pan.
    • As the butter melts, use a spoon to baste the steak (scoop up the butter and pour it over the steak repeatedly). This adds flavor and helps the steak cook more evenly.
  6. Cook to Desired Doneness:

    Use the touch method or a meat thermometer to datermine the doneness of your steak:

    • Rare: 120-130°F (49-54°C) 2-3 minutes per side
    • Medium-rare: 130-135°F (54-57°C) 3-4 minutes per side
    • Medium: 140-145°F (60-63°C) 4-5 minutes per side
    • Medium-well: 150-155°F (66-68°C) 5-6 minutes per side
    • Well-done: 160-170°F (71-77°C) 6-7 minutes per side


  7. Rest the Steak:
    • Once cooked to your desired doneness, transfer the steak to a plate and cover loosely with foil. Let it rest for 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the steak, making it juicier.
  8. Serve:
    • Slice the steak against the grain (this makes the meat more tender) and serve with your favorite side dishes like mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad.

Tips:

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan: If you're cooking multiple steaks, cook them one at a time to ensure a proper sear.
  • Use a thermometer for perfect results if you're unsure about the doneness.
  • Letting it rest is crucial to keeping the steak juicy. Don’t skip this step!

Enjoy your perfectly cooked, flavorful beef steak!
