How to prepare the perfect
Itaian's Spaghetti



Ingredient

Cooking spaghetti is simple, but a few tips can help you achieve the perfect texture. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti (or your preferred amount)
  • Water (enough to fill a large pot)
  • 1-2 tablespoons salt (for seasooning the water)
  • Olive oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil Water: Fill a large pot with plenty of water (about 4–6 quarts) to ensure the pasta has enough room to move around while cooking. Place the pot on high heat and bring the water to a rolling boil.
  2. Add Salt: Gently drop the spaghetti into the boiling water. If the spaghetti is too long to fit, you can press it down with a spoon as it softens or break it in half (although traditionally, spaghetti is cooked whole). Stir the spaghetti immediately after adding it to prevent it from sticking together.
  3. Add Spaghtti: Gently drop the spaghetti into the boiling water. If the spaghetti is too long to fit, you can press it down with a spoon as it softens or break it in half (although traditionally, spaghetti is cooked whole). Stir the spaghetti immediately after adding it to prevent it from sticking together.
  4. Cook Until Al Dente: Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions, usually around 8–10 minutes for al dente (firm to the bite) texture. Stir occasionally during cooking to keep the pasta from sticking together or to the pot.
  5. Taste for Doneness: About a minute before the suggested cooking time is up, taste the spaghetti. It should be tender but still have a slight firmness in the center (al dente). If it needs more time, continue to cook and taste every 30 seconds until it's perfect for your preference.
  6. Reserve Pasta Water (Optional): Before draining the pasta, scoop out a cup of the starchy pasta water. This can be used to adjust the sauce's consistency later.
  7. Drain the Pasta: Once the spaghetti is cooked to your liking, drain it in a colander. Do not rinse the pasta, as this removes the starch that helps the sauce cling to it. If you are not immediately adding sauce, toss the pasta with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
  8. Combine with Sauce: If using a sauce, transfer the drained spaghetti to the pan with the sauce, and toss to coat evenly. If needed, add a little of the reserved pasta water to thin the sauce and make it stick better to the pasta.

Serving:

Serve the spaghetti immediately with your favorite sauce, cheese, or toppings. Enjoy your perfectly cooked spaghetti!
