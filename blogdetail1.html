The secret tips & tricks to prepare a
perfect Cheesecake for our customers



Ingredients

Making a delicious cheesecake depends on a combination of quality ingredients and proper technique. Here are the key ingredients that can elevate the taste:

  1. Crust: 1 ½ cups (150g) graham cracker crumbs (or digestive biscuits), ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar, ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted.
  2. Cheesecake filling: 4 (8oz/225g each) blocks full-fat cream cheese, softened, 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar, 1 tsp vanilla extract, 4 large eggs, room temperature, 1 cup (240ml) sour cream, 1 tsp lemon juice (optional), 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (optional, helps to prevent cracks).

Instructions

Creating the perfect Cheesecake is an art, combining ingredients, techniques, and passion to craft dishes that truly delight the palate. Today, we'll unveil some closely guarded secrets and insider tips for mastering these beloved staples of the culinary world.

  • Prepare the crust: Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C). In a bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until evenly moistened. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a springform pan (9 inches/23 cm). Bake for 8–10 minutes, then let cool while preparing the filling.
  • Prepare the filling: In the large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese onn medium speed until smooth and creamy (avoid overbeating). Add the sugar and continue beating until fully combined. Mix in the vanilla extract and lemon juice. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating on low speed until just combined. Avoid overmixing after adding the eggs, as it can cause cracks. Finally, fold in the sour cream (and flour, if using).
  • Assemble the cheesecake: Water bath method: Wrap the outside of the springform pan tightly in two layers of aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in. Place the cheesecake pan in a large roasting pan. Pour hot water into the roasting pan until it reaches halfway up the sides of the cheesecake pan. Bake for 55–70 minutes, or until the center is slightly jiggly but the edges are set.
  • Cool and chill: Turn off the oven and crack the door open slightly. Let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hiur to prevent cracks. Remove the cheesecake from the water bath and allow it to cool completely at room temperature. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for the texture.
  • Serve: Remove the cheesecake from the pan and top with your favorite toppings (fruit, whipped cream, etc.). Slice with a clean, sharp knife for each cut, wiping the knite with a damp cloth between slices.
